Why Pomegranate, exactly?

Pomegranate is a backend web framework, designed to produce APIs and entire sites that are easy to reason about, easy to maintain, and easy to host. It doesn't dictate how your front end should be written and it doesn't dictate how and where data should be stored. Its powerful Dependency Injection framework allows you to design models and controllers that integrate seamlessly into the rest of the framework.

Key Features

Intuitive structure

Routes, models and controllers are recursively loaded for you, just drop your files into the correct directory (or configure it to use your own). Route files are saved to the path they will be found via url, so files are easy to find.

Dependency Injection

Inversion of control makes it simple to gain access to your controllers, models and custom dependencies. Inside controllers and route functions, your dependencies are simply a parameter away. Nothing to require or pass around, Pomegranate provides you with what you need, where you need it.

Sweet Data modeling

Out of the box support for most SQL servers, using Sequelize as the ORM. CouchDb support through Nano. Redis via, well Redis. Don't like any of those? Install and register your own with the injector and they will be available to you in your controllers and routes... Like Magic!

Merged controllers

Controllers are also managed with dependency injection, so it is trivial to include multiple data sources in your controllers. Outside libraries you add are available to your controllers in exactly the same way as the ones included in Pomegranate.

Ultra Flexible Middleware

Take 100% control of your middleware stack, use our defaults, or simple add the ones you need. Pomegranate makes it simple.

Event Based Logging

Attach handlers to the main Pomegranate object to listen to events related to Platform logs, Request logs, and Errors, with metadata. You choose where they go.
