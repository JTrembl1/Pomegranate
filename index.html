Ok, the known universe is a really big place.

You got us, Pomegranate may not be the most flexible in the entire universe. But it is pretty flexible. It is rediculously easy to use, while at the same time giving you the power to build whatever you need. With an intuitive structure, and a very low noise installation, you can have a project up and running in just a few lines of code. You can check it out on Github here.

Features

  • Transparent model, controller and route loading, just drop your files into the correct directory (or configure it to use your own). More about file loading.
  • Inversion of control makes it simple to gain access to your controllers, models, and custom dependencies. More about Dependencies.
  • Out of the box support for most SQL servers, using Sequelize as the ORM. CouchDb support through Nano. Redis via, well Redis. Don't like any of those? Install and register your own and they will be available to you in your controllers and routes... Magic! More about Models.
  • Named controllers are available in any route, and can even use your own outside dependencies. More about Controllers.
  • Sane default middlewares, with the ability to easily add your own. More about Middleware.
  • Event based logging, so you choose what goes where. More about Logging.
